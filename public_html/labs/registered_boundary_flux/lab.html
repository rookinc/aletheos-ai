Boundary Flux Renderer

Instructions

Use this lab as a boundary-flux renderer. It shows a constrained boundary loading, compressing, focusing, releasing, and leaving trace.

  1. Choose a regime from the Regime menu.
  2. Use PREV and NEXT to step through the phase grammar.
  3. Use PAUSE to freeze the renderer for inspection.
  4. Tune Boundary, Interior, Flux, Emission, and Trace.
  5. Drag the renderer to rotate it. Pinch to zoom.
  6. Adjust Sheets/sec to advance the Cube Register through a P900-style sheet clock.
  7. K900 action binding is inspectable below the renderer, but it is not rendered into the cube yet.

Cube Register roles: top and bottom faces are polarity caps; side faces are relationship walls; edges and corners are coupling seams; the interior is the registered carrier volume.

Boundary: this is a visual grammar only. It is not a hydrodynamic solver and not a physical-force simulation. The Cube Register is a simplified face projection, not the theorem object.
